Munich, Germany

Munich is the third-largest city in Germany and the capital city of the State of Bavaria. Even though excavations prove a much longer history, Munich as such was first mentioned in documents in 1158. Since that time, it has been the residence of numerous dukes, kings, and even an emperor. This history still shapes the face of Munich today in the form of numerous buildings, gardens, churches, and monuments. Museums, theaters, beer gardens and amusement parks round out the recreational offerings, not to mention the attractions of Munich's surrounding countryside. Munich is a place of culture and offers a cosmopolitan climate in which numerous international companies, especially from the IT sector, have settled. With four universities and numerous research institutes, Munich is also a center for research and development. We are looking forward to your visit in Munich during COSADE 2023.

Visa information

Letters from conference organization

International registrants should be particularly aware and careful about visa requirements, and should plan travel well in advance. Please send your request for a letter in support of a visa application to m [dot] pehl [at] tum [dot] de. Use the subject line "COSADE 2023 Visa" and include your name, mailing address, and fax number. Also make sure to indicate which embassy/consulate you are applying to. Authors of accepted papers/posters/etc. should also include the title.

Support letters for non-authors

Due to the high volume of fraudulent visa requests in recent years, a request for a letter in support of a visa application from non-authors must be accompanied by

  1. a statement of interest, motivating the requestor’s interest in the conference topic, as well as
  2. a brief resume, demonstrating a corresponding professional/academic background in related areas

Please contact us first with the above information before registering.
